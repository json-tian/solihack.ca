About Us

Soliac Association

Soliac Association is a new non-profit youth group who strive to develop relationships through tournament-based events. We started off as a high school eSports club at Chinguacousy Secondary School, then expanded due to the high number of requests from students in the area to participate in our tournaments. Now, we focus on using fun competitions in order to create friendships and bonds within our community, as well as put smiles on everyoneâ€™s faces.

The Hackathon

We believe in the importance of hackathons and their significance in bringing people together. Having experienced the stress of school ourselves, we want to ensure that these hardworking students have opportunities to relax and enjoy themselves while showcasing their talents and skills in their community. SoliHack is a 24-hour hackathon featuring a variety of workshops, activities, and competition. At the end, all the participating teams will pitch their projects in front of the judges and compete for awards and prizes. With free food, fun activities and perfect networking opportunities, you won't want to miss SoliHack!

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a hackathon?

Simply put, a hackathon is an invention and innovation marathon. Participants, or hackers, come together to fabricate a project from scratch. There will be mentors and other resources in place which will help you during this emotional journey. Across the duration of the event, hackers will have the opportunity to participate in various fun activities, and will be provided free food and various merchandise. Towards the end, participants will present their projects in front of the community and our judges in hopes of winning amazing prizes!

How much does it cost to attend?

SoliHack is a completely free event! All of the food, great merchandise and amazing people at the entire event are free of charge.

Am I eligible to participate?

If you're in high school, university, or recently graduated university, you're eligible to participate in our event.

Are there any rules I need to follow?

All participants will be expected to adhere to the MLH code of conduct, the MLH Contest Terms and Conditions, and the MLH Privacy Policy.

What if I don't know how to code?

Not a problem! SoliHack is an excellent opportunity to learn something new! Our mentors and workshops will ensure that even if you've never coded before, you'll easily develop the skills you need to create and submit a project that you are proud of!

What should I bring?

Be sure to bring your laptop, laptop charger, and some valid student or government ID. You can also bring a sleeping bag, your comfiest pajamas, a power strip and a drive to learn!

How will teams work?

You'll have plenty of chances to find team members through facebook and slack in the time leading up to the event. We will also have team formation activities on the day of the event, during which you can form your team of up to 4 people. Your team must have at least 3 members to qualify for prizing.

How do I get there?

There are many buses that run across Brampton which can be used to arrive at our event. We are looking into providing buses from Toronto to Brampton, but currently do not have any finalized plans to provide it. We will not be providing any other travel reimbursements for participants.

Can I work on my hack before the event?

To maintain competitive integrity and fairness, participants are not allowed to work on their projects prior to the official start of the event, and are also not allowed to work on pre-existing projects. However, we encourage you to spend some time on your own refining your skills before the event. You'll also be able to borrow or bring your own hardware as needed.
